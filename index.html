Computational Tools for Better Engineering Education

I want to incorporate computing into undergraduate mechanical engineering curricula to both strengthen traditional lessons and create transferable software skills for students in their post-undergraduate careers.

The National Academy of Engineering wrote a book in 2004 (The Engineer of 2020: Visions of Engineering in the New Century), in which they highlighted the unprecedented evolution of engineering and the growing need for engineers to adapt to new problems. To address this, they argued that engineering education was due for massive reform. While traditional programs were designed to prepare students for the problems of the time, modern programs need to equip students with the tools to solve problems that don't even exist. That is a monumental task. I believe that we can solve this by infusing undergraduate courses with both raw computing and simulation demonstrations. I know that these things can be used as powerful educational tools to both reinforce theoretical concepts and empower students to dive even deeper into multidisciplinary engineering. I have already implemented this vision once with a new course in the mechanical engineering curriculum at Pitt. I learned a lot about designing an effective curriculum, and I am excited to put those things into practice in more classes in the future.
