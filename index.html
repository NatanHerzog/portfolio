Who I am

BPhil Candidate in Engineering Science at the University of Pittsburgh

I love understanding how the world around me works, and engineering is a gateway to building a more robust view of some of the less-intuitive things surrounding us every day.

All throughout undergrad, I found enjoyment in the ways that my understanding developed in classes. Talking about stress concentrations and fatigue put math to things that have long made sense. And simultaneously, Maxwell's equations (for example) provide us with a (tantalizingly asymmetric) description of electromagnetics, which is otherwise much more difficult to understand because we cannot directly perceive it.

So, in a manner of speaking, engineering is one path to overcoming the inherent limitations in our intuition about the world. Especially with the relatively new power of simulation, we now have the ability to observe so many more complex phenomena and to begin to develop our intuition for the otherwise imperceptible things in life.
